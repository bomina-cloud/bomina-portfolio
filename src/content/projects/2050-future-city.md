## Methodology

2050 scenarios were developed using the **Morphological Box Method**, forecasting domain-level shifts in values (leisure, society, economy) and foundations (governance, infrastructure). UN-Habitat's City Prosperity Index (CPI) was applied to classify 30 global cities into five data-driven city archetypes, enabling differentiated business-model development per archetype.

Phased new-business roadmaps were built for 2025, 2035, and 2050, yielding **15 integrated mobility-energy business models** tailored to each city archetype. Mobility evolution was framed across four stages:

> "service diversification → payment integration → platform expansion → convergence with other infrastructures"

Differentiated transport architectures were structured for each city type, linking services, hardware, platforms, and infrastructure through shared data flows. Renewable and distributed-energy value-chain opportunities were identified at the consumer and retail levels.

## Validation & workshops

A **Delphi workshop** was organized in Singapore (Feb 2019) with nine global urban experts — including leaders from UN-Habitat, 100 Resilient Cities, and energy policy advisors — to validate future scenarios, refine city-type characteristics, and identify key opportunities and risks across mobility and energy domains.

A **group-wide backcasting workshop** with 50 Hyundai engineers (May 2019) converted 2050 scenarios into actionable 2035 and 2025 development roadmaps across all executing departments. Department-specific guidance documents connected current R&D tasks to 2035/2050 end-goals, later formalized as part of the execution framework.

## Six core mobility opportunity areas

Linked to urban futures and connected back to 2035/2050 end-goals for each executing department:

1. On-demand mobility solutions
2. Multi-purpose Purpose Built Vehicle (PBV) development
3. Proprietary energy solutions
4. Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X)-based on-road journey innovation
5. Seamless multimodal & payment systems
6. Region-based open-innovation hubs

## The Hub Solution (CES 2020)

Designed as an alternative transport infrastructure for high-density cities by linking Urban Air Mobility (UAM) and PBV into a seamless multimodal transfer hub. Addresses the constraints of aging, high-density cities (e.g., San Francisco) through a modular hub that enhances commuting efficiency and supports new urban lifestyles.

![Hub Solution aerial view — UAM landing pad with PBV modules circulating to street level](/images/projects/2050-future-city/hub-solution-aerial.jpg)

UX journeys, PBV spatial concepts, and the integrated UAM–PBV–Hub ecosystem narrative were created in collaboration with global design centers. Keynote planning for the **Mobility Insight Forum (MIF) 2019** in San Francisco was led in parallel, with the vision presented again at CES 2020.

![Hub interior PBV modules — adaptive cabin scenarios across living, work, and transit](/images/projects/2050-future-city/hub-interior-panels.png)

Simulation-based smart-city and hub-infrastructure models demonstrated potential to improve urban travel times by approximately **30–45%** and transfer efficiency by around **25%**, positioning the Hub Solution as a validated mitigation strategy for congestion and commuting bottlenecks.

## Hexagon City Structure

The **Hexagon City Structure** — Hyundai's greenfield smart-city master model — was derived from the A1 city archetype, defining a hexagonal cell-based urban layout that separates pedestrian/green public space at grade from mobility/logistics/energy infrastructure underground, and integrates PBV and UAM as core connectors between cells.

## Strategic ripple effects

- Internal: vision-sharing sessions to 400+ employees embedded the future-city framework and mobility/energy roadmap as a shared organizational reference.
- External: model presented to the EU Commission, Korean National Assembly, and national think tanks. Contributed to MOUs with cities such as Singapore and partners in the Middle East, where the framework has been used as a baseline for greenfield smart-city and logistics-hub designs.
- Hyundai Motor Group smart-city standardization: adopted as the reference for PBV/UAM/Hub ecosystem planning. Informed the **Uber Elevate collaboration (2020)**, **Arrival investment (~EUR 100M, 2020)**, and the establishment of **Supernal (2021)**.
