## Strategic posture

The program shifts biomimicry inside Hyundai Motor Group from being a **conceptual design tool** into a **practical innovation engine** — one that systematically applies nature-inspired principles to complex mobility challenges in lightweight materials, sustainability, and AI-based UX design.

## Three-phase validation process

Designed a structured pipeline translating biological inspiration into production-ready concepts:

1. **Concept Ideation** — biological models mapped to mobility challenges.
2. **Technical Verification** — engineering feasibility testing with 3D modeling, simulation, and AI-assisted visualization.
3. **Product Development** — integration into CES concept models and future vehicle programs.

## Governance: One Team model

Established a cross-functional governance model integrating **Design, R&D, Manufacturing, and Material Divisions**, with executive approval for a three-year dedicated Task Force. The TF governance and knowledge pipeline ensure iterative data exchange across divisions, resolving organizational silos and embedding biomimicry practices into the corporate R&D workflow.

## Workshops & strategic vision

### Biomimicry Vision Workshop (May 2025)

50+ cross-disciplinary participants convened to derive three strategic vision themes:

- **SIMBIOTIC Mobility**
- **Responsive AI**
- **Visible Safety Solutions**

### Technical Workshop (Oct 2025)

50+ R&D engineers transformed biological models into **20+ engineering features and prototypes** using 3D modeling, simulation, and AI-assisted visualization tools.

## Feature prototypes

Bio-inspired features currently in development:

- **Bending seat** — structural principle from pill-bug segmented exoskeleton, enabling adaptive curvature without mechanical hinges.
- **Lightweight bumper** — geometry derived from insect-wing cross-section, optimizing stiffness-to-weight ratio.
- **Energy-absorbing landing pad** — feline-paw mechanics applied to impact absorption for high-mobility applications.

Prototype implementation and feasibility validation are advanced through design–engineering collaboration, linked to **CES 2027 concept model presentations**.

## Sustainability index

Introduced an **AI-driven sustainability index system** measuring:

- Lightweight ratio
- Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) efficiency
- Material recyclability

…to evaluate each concept's long-term environmental and business impact.

## Organizational capability

Developed and led **Biomimicry Thinking training modules for 50+ R&D and design members**, strengthening the organization's capacity to translate biological inspiration into engineering design language.

## External partnerships

Collaborated with **RISD Nature Lab** to evolve Hyundai's seven-year biomimicry partnership into a corporate-level innovation program, expanding from design inspiration to technical proof-of-concept and commercialization readiness.

Co-led the **Kia Interior Vision Model** development, applying nature-based sensory interaction principles — light, sound, texture — to create an emotionally responsive interior concept aligned with SDV and future HMI trends.

## CES 2027 narrative

Preparing the presentation strategy for Hyundai's first biomimicry-based mobility showcase, integrating concept storytelling, sustainability metrics, and visual narratives.
