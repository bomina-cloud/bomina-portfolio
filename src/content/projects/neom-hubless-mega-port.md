## Project context

Conventional deep-sea ports route cargo through nine sequential steps from vessel unloading to off-dock warehouse intake, producing repeated loading/unloading cycles, inter-stage movements, and substantial queueing delays. For NEOM — a greenfield smart city with a target of 2,500 containers/day at OXAGON — this architecture would bottleneck the entire city-scale logistics vision.

As Senior Manager in Hyundai Motor Group's Open Innovation Strategy Team, I led the cross-functional program that translated a design-driven concept into a real-world logistics system, coordinating Hyundai's U.S. Design & Technical Center, hydrogen/energy and autonomous technology divisions, global strategy organizations, and external partners including port-operations experts and logistics operators.

## System architecture

I developed the full logistics value-chain model for next-generation autonomous freight operations, mapping the flow from port-side cargo handling and automated routing to mid-mile hub-to-hub transport and inland fulfillment integration. The architecture is organized as a three-tier end-to-end logistics model:

1. **Port Integration Zone** — Hub-less Mega Port acting as the foundational first node.
2. **Hub-to-Hub Autonomous Middle-Mile Zone** — modular separation and recombination across intermediate hubs.
3. **Customized Fulfillment & Last-Mile Zone** — trailer drone devices adapting to inland demand patterns.

KPIs and functional roles were defined for each segment, with cargo-flow rates, automation ratios, throughput potential, and peak-condition capacity modeled against OXAGON's projected 2,500 containers/day.

## The Hub-less Mega Port concept

The spiral-based underground port–fulfillment system eliminates traditional bottlenecks by integrating all critical processes into a continuous, automation-driven vertical cargo flow. Cargo unloading, sorting, customs, quarantine, and fulfillment occur simultaneously as freight moves vertically through the spiral structure, removing intermediate logistics nodes and enabling continuous flow from ports to inland hubs.

![Spiral Hubless Port — system structure and container flow logic](/images/projects/neom-trailer-drone/spiral-hubless-port.jpg)

I directed simulation-based feasibility studies and developed quantifiable logistics performance indicators, built in Blender and the SIGMA program. Use-case-specific technical parameters — spiral diameter, gradient angle, underground depth, conveyor-belt width, load criteria, and the trailer drone's dimensional profile, payload capacity, and operating envelope — were optimized through rapid expert-validated iteration. System-level vehicle integration was implemented through Alias to ensure seamless connectivity with the broader mobility ecosystem.

## Autonomous trailer drone

The trailer drone is an energy-efficient 4×2 driverless transport platform with a modular, swappable architecture deployable across logistics, port operations, airport services, construction, and other industrial sectors. I defined the hardware specifications needed for deployment within the Hub-less Mega Port system — size constraints, Side-Loading interface requirements, docking logic, operational envelopes, mid-mile routing behavior, and module coupling/decoupling conditions — and developed initial device orchestration logic for how multiple devices (trailers, e-bogies, modules, cargo units) interact across nodes.

## Strategic impact

- **Executive alignment at Hyundai**: elevated the project to group-wide initiative status, establishing the Hub-less Mega Port and trailer drone integration as a core pillar of Hyundai's mid- to long-term logistics and commercial mobility strategy.
- **NEOM partnership**: spearheaded a two-day leadership workshop in Tabuk, Saudi Arabia (Aug 2022) with executives including the former UPS North America CEO, iteratively refining the concept to align with NEOM's industrial "Backbone" connecting OXAGON Port, Hyperloop rail lines, and The Line megacity.
- **Platform architecture**: defined the strategic blueprint for Hyundai's 2028 X-MMP (future Mobility commercial Modular Platform), ensuring simulation results, logistics use-cases, modular device behaviors, and system integration requirements were embedded into the platform's initial product architecture.
- **Downstream logistics strategy**: learnings directly informed Hyundai's future hydrogen-enabled logistics strategy, including hydrogen-based freight systems at HMGMA (Georgia) and the expansion of autonomous logistics services for North American supply-chain networks.

## Context analysis

U.S. logistics and port bottlenecks — port congestion, labor shortages, limited yard scalability, and pandemic-driven disruptions — were quantified using U.S. Bureau of Transportation Statistics and Federal Reserve analyses, positioning the Hub-less Mega Port and trailer drone architecture as a strategic solution for supply-chain resilience, reshoring, and nearshoring logistics policy.
