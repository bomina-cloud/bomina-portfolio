## Strategic framework

Analyzed how AI would reshape each domain of Hyundai's value chain through 2035, developed future scenarios, and built **2020 / 2025 / 2030 technical roadmaps** for the automotive value chain — then derived concrete action items for manufacturing, engineering, product planning, in-vehicle user experience, and mobility services.

All enabling technologies were classified into:

- **Applied AI Capabilities** (user-facing and functional AI)
- **Supporting AI Infrastructure** (data, compute, MLOps, integration)

Dependencies and linkages were defined as part of an OEM-wide AI technology architecture, co-developed and validated with engineers from Element AI in Canada.

## Strategic AI PoCs

Executed detailed technical roadmaps with Element AI and Affectiva, integrating AI features into concept vehicles for CES 2019. For each use case, I defined the required data sets, matched optimal internal business units with external startups, specified project requirements and technical needs, secured structured data for AI algorithm development, and established the necessary training environments.

## R.E.A.D (Real-time Emotion Adaptive Driving)

Led the optimization of Affectiva's emotion-recognition system for in-car UX, developing a real-time emotion-adaptive system that recognizes driver and passenger facial expressions and adjusts the vehicle environment accordingly.

Built a **six-step R.E.A.D operation scenario** and implemented an in-car UX that dynamically personalizes:

- Content recommendation
- Climate control
- Ambient lighting
- Voice assistant behavior

…all based on detected emotional states. Showcased at **CES 2019**.

![R.E.A.D concept vehicle at CES 2019 — Kia booth installation showcasing the emotion-adaptive cabin](/images/projects/ai-future-strategy/ces2019-read-vehicle.webp)

## Global AI network conference

Organized a large-scale AI global scholar and industry network conference inside Hyundai Motor Group, attended by **~5 group presidents, ~70 executives, and ~250 employees**.

Guru-level AI experts invited to lead value-chain-focused focus-group discussions:

- **Andrew Ng** (Landing AI)
- **Philippe Beaudoin** (Element AI)
- **Eric Xing** (Petuum)
- **André König** (City AI New York)

Complex AI technology stacks were translated into visual, scenario-based narratives so C-level leaders could understand and make strategic decisions. The conference directly led to follow-on collaborations with Landing AI and Element AI on AI roadmaps, PoCs, and in-vehicle USP development.

![AI × R.E.A.D (CES 2019) — value proposition, 2030 outlook scenarios, technology roadmap, and Hyundai action items](/images/projects/ai-future-strategy/ai-read-roadmap.jpg)

## Production impact

AI-driven concepts flowed back into vehicle programs and USPs — multiple AI features were further developed into unique selling propositions for concept vehicles. Design-evaluation sessions integrated several of these features into the IONIQ 5 concept model, contributing to decisions that shaped the final production steering-wheel design.
