Doctoral dissertation. Identified the structural limitations of traditional closed R&D models in large automotive firms and explained why open-innovation ecosystems are essential for accelerating breakthrough innovation.

- Proposed an integrated **Innovation Execution Structure** that connects external partners, internal R&D, decision-making units, and strategic management layers to improve idea flow and reduce organizational bottlenecks.
- Presented a step-by-step execution model — ranging from opportunity sensing and co-creation to validation and internal scaling — supported by case studies from leading global innovation organizations.
- Demonstrated how open-innovation practices enable automotive companies to transform future-scenario concepts into implementable strategies, enhancing long-term competitiveness and organizational adaptability.
