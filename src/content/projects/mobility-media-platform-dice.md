## From consumer foresight to a business model

The project began not as a design-first ideation but as foresight research into post-smartphone digital-native users (born after 2009), identified as the dominant mobility segment for 2030–2035 with a demand for participatory, streaming-based, and context-aware media consumption. Full in-transit behavior mapping produced the **Creatorship-driven Open Platform** opportunity space — the conceptual foundation for Hyundai's media-centric UX strategy.

From that foundation, I developed the world's first media-specialized robotaxi business model — a revenue-driven service architecture where vehicle structure, UX, and immersive media functions are dictated by monetization logic rather than styling. MMP was established as a scalable robotaxi media ecosystem of three monetizable service modules:

1. **Immersive music subscription**
2. **Journey-based video sharing**
3. **Real-to-virtual linked gaming**

Each has distinct operational logic, content requirements, and media engagement pathways, structured into a tiered monetization model (Basic, Premium, Premier+) where price tier maps to immersion level, device complexity, and content type — integrating Augmented Reality (AR), 360° Motion-Based Virtual Reality (VR) Control, and the Metaverse Gate interface.

## Economic viability with Roland Berger

Built the full economic and operational viability model for media-specialized robotaxis with Roland Berger:

- **U.S. market potential**: ~USD 160M as robotaxi penetration expands beyond 50% of urban mobility.
- **Unit economics (2030 scenario)**: ~USD 255K annual revenue, ~USD 30K operating profit, ~11% margin per vehicle.

## Technical foundation with KAIST & partners

Defined the algorithmic and backend foundation for journey-based media personalization with KAIST AI, resulting in two context-adaptive engines:

- **AR Journey Mate®** — real-time route, weather, environmental cue, and preference-driven AR overlays.
- **Sync Music®** — context-adaptive music engine.

Global media innovation partnerships included Disney StudioLAB (Burbank), Spotify, and CJ ENM, with co-development workshops validating content scenarios, immersive media formats, and vehicle-specific rendering methods.

## From concept to vehicle — DICE at CES 2024

I served as experience lead and chief architect for DICE (Digital Curated Experience), transforming MMP from a service model into a physical vehicle equipped with transparent OLED displays, spatial audio, metaverse-entry interfaces, and hyper-personalized single-passenger cabin design.

The project direction shifted from an early two-seat bidirectional layout to a one-seat personalized **media mobility device**, redefining DICE as Hyundai's flagship immersive mobility concept at CES 2024. MMP was then integrated into Hyundai's Software-Defined Vehicle (SDV) ecosystem, defined as a service and UX layer running on the Pleos Vehicle OS, and presented in CEO-level strategy forums where it contributed to Hyundai's official CES 2024 SDV vision.

## Cabin architecture

Redefined Level-5 robotaxi interior architecture through:

- Multisurface transparent OLED displays
- Spatial audio matrices
- Context-adaptive lighting and glass
- Creator-linked smart windows
- Immersive AR/MR content experiences

Core entertainment-based USP concepts were extended toward next-generation Genesis and IONIQ pre-mass-production platforms (2030 target launch) through collaboration with Hyundai Motor America, aligning vehicle requirements, commercialization strategy, and SDV-integration pathways.
