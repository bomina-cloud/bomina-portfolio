## 10-year technology simulation

Developed an integrated future-vehicle technology simulation that unified individually scattered emerging technologies into a coherent vehicle-system perspective. Delivered user-centered technology scenarios to R&D teams, defined inter-technology linkages, and provided 10-year technology direction and specification documents identifying core concepts requiring early development.

Co-led future-technology validation with Hyundai Motor Group's Central Research Institute, deriving **20 future technology items** and jointly building the **HMG Technology Roadmap (TRM)**. Created a formal TRM guideline defining tech components, external maturity levels, internal research status, and the 5–10 year development path required for each item.

## Cultural foresight with GQ

Conducted global cultural-trend foresight by identifying subcultural codes that will scale into mainstream culture over the next decade:

- Recruited **60+ Cultural Pioneers** in Korea and North America.
- Developed a cultural-technology interpretation framework with **GQ Magazine** and **Chicago Innovation Center**.
- Executed immersive qualitative research with cultural creators through field ethnography and in-depth interviews.

Covert small-group roundtables were organized in symbolic cultural districts (Itaewon, etc.), exploring topics including "hidden future technologies," "luxury behaviors," "consumer cultural following," and "cultural shifts & consumption."

A **Grounded Theory** coding process extracted core cultural codes. Global expansion came through partnerships with Chicago Innovation Center and Europe's 21st-Century Research Center.

## Technological Humanization

Predicted future consumer cultural orientation and converted insights into **nine human-centered emotional-tech concepts** linked to autonomous services, sustainable materials/energy, and next-generation HMI. Conducted joint workshops with MIT Mobile Experience Lab (Prof. Federico Casalegno) to create cultural-technology scenarios and define emotional technology roadmaps.

Introduced the concept of **"Technological Humanization"** into vehicle UX and proposed emotion- and context-adaptive display systems.

## Panoramic Infotainment System

Led the development of the **Panoramic Infotainment System** (now mass-produced in Genesis models), enabling real-time weather/season-responsive visual environments and emotionally immersive display transitions.

Created **digital Cover-Skin UX (Mood Mode)**, where icons adapt to temperature, ice density, and other contextual cues — a concept paralleling Apple's later ICE UX approach.

## Hyper Controller System

Designed the **Hyper Controller System** for multi-modal interaction:

- Gesture scroll / zoom / hover
- Handwriting input
- Digitalized textures

Explored tactile digitalization using artificial skin + nano-wire microstructures to mimic human touch.

## Emotional-UX ecosystem

Extended the ecosystem through:

- Interactive displays
- Steering-wheel note-taking
- Device hyper-connectivity
- New digital interaction metaphors

## Validation & production rollout

Produced high-fidelity UX videos to brief Hyundai's advanced development divisions, then built a full **Future Technology BUCK (2014)** to visualize and validate technologies. All concepts underwent feasibility testing with the Central Research Institute and were later applied to Genesis, Hyundai, and Kia flagship models.

Supported the development of the **IONIQ Interactive Pixel Light**, enabling emotion-driven ambient lighting and intuitive vehicle-user feedback — including welcome animations, speed-linked mood lighting, and real-time emotional ambience.
