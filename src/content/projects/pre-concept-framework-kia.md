## Problem framing

Led advanced **Product Concept Committee (PCC)** and early concept planning for Kia Carnival, Sorento, and K5, and identified the structural limitation of the traditional PCC process (M-24) — where abstract styling concepts were proposed without functional or technical pre-development, often resulting in weak carry-over into production.

## The Pre-Concept stage

Proposed and designed a new **"Pre-Concept" stage positioned at M-30** (the front-end of Long-Range Product Planning), enabling designers, engineers, and planners to collaboratively generate **function- and technology-led ideas before styling begins**.

Validated the methodology by collaborating with **Aalto University, Helsinki**, developing a function-driven advanced concept framework and applying it experimentally to the **YP (2nd-generation Carnival) program**. The design team proposed consumer-driven USPs that were integrated early into platform and package development.

## Six-step innovation workflow

Structured the Pre-Concept stage as a systematic innovation workflow:

1. **Hypothesis Setting**
2. **Opportunity Discovery**
3. **Opportunity Expansion**
4. **Idea Structuring**
5. **Solution Exploration**
6. **Solution Validation**

…enabling technical and functional ideas to be incorporated into product and technology targets before formal design sketching.

## Application to the YP program

Applied and tested the Pre-Concept process (Jan–Aug 2010) with interior and exterior designers for the YP program, embedding future life-stage changes (4 years ahead of launch) and evolving MPV usage scenarios.

## Consumer-centric interior USPs

Developed a portfolio of consumer-driven interior innovations:

- **Conversational overhead console**
- **Analog-inspired panorama sunroof**
- **Removable center seat / flexible cargo layout**
- **Detachable IT-device interfaces**
- **Multi-purpose center table configuration**
- **Baby-care accessibility from 1st/2nd row**
- **Flexible "Flex Rail" console system**
- New ideas for family-based seat interaction and space adaptability

## Cross-cultural validation

Conducted user validation with **Hyundai Motor America** — engaging engineers, planners, and designers to test concept relevance against North American "soccer mom" behaviors, identify gaps, and refine concepts prior to design freeze.

## Institutional impact

- The success of the 2nd-generation Carnival (YP) validated the Pre-Concept methodology.
- Kia expanded MPV market opportunities on the back of the methodology.
- The framework later informed Kia's **Purpose Built Vehicle (PBV) strategy**.
- The Pre-Concept stage was subsequently adopted into the official **M-30 development process**, institutionalizing the strategic role of design within Hyundai–Kia.
